Typography Task

Troughout all my posters I used green and the font Clarendon URW
Axis poster
For this poster I had to create an axial design, this consits of all the content being on an invisible line. I decide to tilt my contetn instead of having it all straight down as I think it draws more attention to it. I added some shapes such as cirlces and rectangles to create a more interesting layout.
Bilateral poster
For this poster it was Bilateral desing. It is based on centering everything and making it balanced. I try used some symmetry with the sqaures to create this balance. Once again I decided to put some of it at an angle, as having everything straight can look sort of boring.
Dilatational poster
I started of this poster by creating points for the text to go around. Instead of having blank spaces I added in cirlces. I spent the most time on this one, playing aorund with the arc's and how to create a spiral.
Grid poster
This is classic design which is seen in everyday life, Grid design is all very straight and blocky. All the text and content are all fitted nicely into the boxes and creates a very structured and neat look.
Modular poster
There are many ways to create a modular layout, I used circles to to show a repeating structure in the poster. I didn't type inside the circles but used circle for information on the poster and clearly shows the information needed for the viewer.
Radial poster
For radial layout everything has to be coming from or pointing onto one certain point. I've created three focal points, one large one for the titl and the information that is needed first and two small ones.
Random poster
For this poster I did a combination of all layouts to create a random layout. With a few shapes and the text in different angles. Even though it is random I feel that is it still quite aesthetic and clear to understand.
transitonal
Personally this was the most confusing layout to design, having the waves transitioning and flowing into eachother was pretty challening. I used lines to show the movement of the text.
